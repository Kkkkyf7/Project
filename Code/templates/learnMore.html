Cataract

Cloudy appearance on the lens causing blurred vision, glare, and faded colors. Can lead to vision loss if untreated, especially in older adults.

Detailed information about Cataract.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Microaneurysms, hemorrhages, and exudates on the retina due to diabetes. Can progress to macular edema or retinal detachment, causing severe vision impairment.

Detailed information about Diabetic Retinopathy.

Glaucoma

Optic nerve damage leading to peripheral vision loss. Increased intraocular pressure damages the optic nerve fibers. If untreated, can cause irreversible blindness.

Detailed information about Glaucoma.

Normal Retina

healthy retina without any visible abnormalities. Consistent monitoring of retinal health is important to detect early signs of potential eye diseases.

Detailed information about Normal Retina.
